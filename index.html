Meal Prep OS v1.1 · What this system is · The weekly workflow · First-time setup order
What is 29 Performance Meal Prep OS?

A single-file kitchen intelligence system for athlete meal prep businesses. Everything — ingredient costs, recipe builds, weekly menus, batch production, staff scheduling, purchasing, and multi-week financial history — lives in one HTML file. No server, no subscription, no internet required. Open it in any modern browser and you have a full operational OS.

All data saves automatically to localStorage (instant, offline-safe) and syncs to Firebase in real time when configured — so Aran and staff always see the same data on any device. Export a JSON backup regularly (sidebar → ⬇ Export) as a secondary safeguard.

The Weekly Workflow — Day by Day

Every week follows the same rhythm. Two weeks are always in play at once: one being fulfilled, one being built for the next cycle.

1
Thursday — Advance the week & start building next  Weekly Menu → ⏭ Advance, then ⚡ Quick Build
After Wednesday's drop is complete, click ⏭ Advance Week on the Weekly Menu. This archives the old week and promotes next week's menu to Current. Then go to ⚡ Quick Build, set Push Target to 🔜 Building Next, and fill in each slot — components, availability, fuel line, sell price, description, photo, and tags all go in the ▾ Details panel. Check the f+p margin row before pushing each dish. Set the week label and drop dates on the Weekly Menu tab.
2
Friday — Open ordering & schedule labour  Weekly Menu portal strip · Labour
Click Open for Orders in the portal strip. Set Sunday and Wednesday order deadlines (e.g. Sat 20:00 / Tue 20:00). Go to Labour and schedule this week's kitchen shifts. Go to Cost Stack to verify margins are healthy — adjust sell prices on the menu if needed.
3
Saturday — Confirm orders & check batch sizes  Orders page
Portal orders arrive with a 🟡 Pending badge. Use Bulk Confirm to accept all at once (or review individually). Check the allocation bar — if any dish shows more orders than planned portions, increase S1 or S2 on that dish. Once ordering closes, click ↓ Sync Portions on the menu to lock portion counts to confirmed order demand.
4
Saturday/Sunday — Purchasing & prep  Purchasing
Go to Purchasing for the ingredient order list — quantities are calculated from your confirmed S1/S2 portion counts. Place your ingredient orders with suppliers. Add any athlete dish variants manually via Orders → + Add Order.
5
Sunday — Cook S1 & deliver  Production · Packaging · Delivery
Filter Production to S1 (Sunday session). Cook batches per the production planner. Pack boxes using the Packaging sheet. Dispatch via the Route Sheet. Print labels from the Labels tab. Mark deliveries confirmed on the route sheet as you go.
6
Tuesday/Wednesday — Cook S2 & deliver  Production · Packaging · Delivery
Repeat the production, packaging, and delivery flow for the S2 (Wednesday) session. Filter all views to S2. Once Wednesday's drop is done, the week is complete — repeat from Step 1.
The rhythm in one sentence: Thursday you advance and build. Friday you open ordering and schedule labour. Saturday you confirm orders. Sunday and Wednesday you cook, pack, and deliver.
First-Time Setup Order
1
Add your ingredients
Go to Ingredients and add every raw ingredient you use. Fill in AP cost per 100g, processing yield %, cook yield %, and all nutrition fields. This is the most important step — every cost and nutrition figure downstream flows from here.
2
Build your recipe components
Go to Recipes and build each component (Protein, Carb, Veg, Condiment, Complete). Quantities are single-serving raw weights in grams. Add cook method, station, timings, and step-by-step instructions.
3
Add your staff roster
Go to Labour and add every team member with their role, hourly rate, and status. Do this once — you'll schedule them week by week using this roster.
4
Set up your cost stack
Go to Cost Stack and update the packaging lines to match your actual box, lid, and sleeve costs. Labour per Dish will auto-fill once you have shifts. Add any overhead or delivery cost lines your business carries.
5
Then follow the weekly workflow above
Once master data is set up, the weekly cycle is: build menu → schedule labour → check costs → run operations → archive.

The Labour → Cost Stack Auto-Sync

When you open the Cost Stack, it reads your current shifts and staff rates, calculates total labour cost ÷ total portions, and updates the Labour per Dish line automatically. This means your cost model always reflects the actual team schedule — not a stale manual figure. The green AUTO badge on the Labour per Dish line confirms it's live-synced. If you need to override it (e.g. for a quote before shifts are scheduled), click ✏ Override to unlock the field, and ↩ Auto to restore sync.

What Updates the Dashboard?

The Dashboard reads live from the current weekly menu every time you visit it. KPIs, alerts, volume chart, and macro profile are all real-time — no cache to clear. If the dashboard looks wrong, the underlying menu or recipe data needs fixing.

Yield % Explained — Worked Examples
Processing Yield (trimming)
Example: Broccoli — 81% processing yield
You buy 1,000g of broccoli.
You trim the stalks and lose 190g.
Usable florets: 810g = 81% of what you paid for.

EP Cost = AP Cost ÷ (Proc% ÷ 100)
If AP = £0.219/100g → EP = £0.219 ÷ 0.81 = £0.270/100g
Cook Yield (moisture loss or absorption)
Example: Chicken Breast — 75% cook yield
You start with 165g raw chicken per serving.
After oven cooking, it loses 25% moisture.
Cooked weight: 165 × 0.75 = 123.75g on the plate.

This is the weight that appears on your Packaging sheet.
Why does rice show 333%? Rice is the exception to the rule. Raw dry rice absorbs water as it cooks, so the cooked weight is significantly heavier than the raw weight. 75g dry basmati + absorbed water ≈ 250g cooked. That's 333% of the raw weight. Enter the cook yield as 333 for rice. The system handles numbers over 100% correctly.
Total Yield
Combined processing + cook yield
Total Yield = (Proc% ÷ 100) × (Cook% ÷ 100)

Broccoli: 0.81 × 0.60 = 0.486 — 130g raw → 63g cooked florets
Rice: 1.00 × 3.33 = 3.33 — 75g raw → 250g cooked rice
Nutrition Fields

All nutrition is entered per 100g raw weight. The system scales values by the quantity used in each recipe line. Core macros (kcal, protein, carbs, fat, fibre, salt) flow into recipe cards and the weekly menu macro display. The extended micronutrient fields (vitamins, minerals) appear in ingredient detail views and the RDA comparison panel.

Use nutrition data from the manufacturer label, Cronometer, or the Open Food Facts database for accuracy. Where exact data isn't available, use a trusted source like the USDA FoodData Central.

Editing and Deleting Ingredients

Click the ✏ pencil icon on any ingredient row to open the edit modal with all current values pre-filled. Click ✕ to delete — note that deleting an ingredient that is used in recipes will cause those recipe lines to show as missing. Always check the Recipe Engine after deleting an ingredient.

The Single-Serving Rule
All recipe quantities are per single serving, in raw grams. The system scales them up to batch size in Production automatically. Never enter batch quantities here — if you change the recipe's serving size, all downstream batch calculations will be wrong.
Duplicating & Versioning Recipes

Use the ⎘ Duplicate button (the copy icon on every recipe row) to open the Recipe Duplicate modal. This is the recommended approach for versioning recipes, creating athlete-specific variants, or trialling macro adjustments without touching your proven baseline.

Athlete variant example: Duplicate your standard Tikka Bowl → tag it Athlete → increase protein quantity → set to Active when ready. The original stays untouched for your general menu.

What the Cooked Weight Column Shows

The cooked weight in the recipe list is the sum of all ingredient lines multiplied by their total yield (processing × cook yield). This is the weight that appears on the Packaging sheet — what physically goes in the box. It is not the raw weight and not a target — it is a calculation.

⚡ Quick Build
The single editing interface for planning and building your menu · Slot structure · Details panel · Push to Weekly Menu
What Quick Build Is For

Quick Build is the primary place to plan, configure, and finalise every dish before it goes live. It is the single interface for all dish editing — components, scales, availability, fuel line, pricing, packaging, tags, photo, and description are all set here. The Weekly Menu tab is read-only by default and should only be used for post-push corrections via its ✎ Edit override.

Rule of thumb: If you're building a new week's menu, stay in Quick Build. Only jump to Weekly Menu to check what's live or to fix a mistake after pushing.
Push Target — Current Week vs Building Next

At the top of Quick Build is a Push Target selector. This decides where your slots go when you click → Menu or → Push All to Menu. Select the slot you're building for before you start filling in dishes:

TargetWhen to use
📦 Current WeekReplacing or adding to the week already in production. A warning appears if active orders exist — be careful, kitchen may already be working from this slot.
🔜 Building NextBuilding the upcoming week in advance while the current week runs. This is the normal mode — push here and the portal automatically shows next week's menu as soon as the first dish lands.

Pushing to the Menu

Once a slot is configured, push it individually with → Menu, or push all unpushed slots with → Push All to Menu in the top-right toolbar. Both buttons respect the active Push Target. After pushing:

  • The slot's push button turns into a green ✓ badge showing which target it went to
  • The dish appears immediately on the Weekly Menu tab (and on the portal if Building Next is selected)
  • The QB slot stays intact — you can still edit it as a reference, but changes won't sync back to the pushed dish automatically
QB and Weekly Menu are not linked after push. Once pushed, the dish on the menu is independent. If you need to change something after pushing, use the ✎ Edit button on the Weekly Menu card — don't push again or you'll create a duplicate.
Menu Library

The Menu Library (right panel in Quick Build) lets you save entire week builds as reusable menus. Click 💾 Save as Menu in the toolbar to snapshot all current slots into a named entry. Each saved menu shows the dish list with portion counts, Sun/Wed totals, and average macros. Reload a saved menu into QB with Load, or deploy it directly to the active Push Target with Deploy — carrying over portions, fuel line, pricing, packaging, and tags. Use the ✏ rename and ⎘ duplicate buttons to manage your library. Menus sync to Firebase so they're available on all devices. Use the ↺ Reset button to clear all slots back to the 12-slot default (pushes and assignments are cleared, but your saved menus are untouched).

Reuse workflow: Build a strong week in QB, save it as a menu, then in future weeks click Deploy to load that exact menu — tweak one or two dishes if needed, then push. Faster than building from scratch every week and ensures consistent macros across repeat dishes.
Component Reuse Panel

Below the slots table, the Component Reuse panel shows how many times each component appears across all slots. Use it to spot accidental repetition (the same protein in multiple slots) or to check that you've balanced the menu across flavour families.

The Weekly Menu has two independent slots, switched via the tabs at the top of the menu panel. You always have two weeks in play simultaneously — one being fulfilled, one being built and opened for ordering:

SlotWhat it isPortal behaviour

Critical Rule — Always Build on the Right Slot

The most common mistake is uploading dish photos or filling in "Serves with" while on the wrong slot. The portal only shows the Building Next slot's dishes — so any photos or details set on the Current Week tab are invisible to customers.

Example of the wrong way vs right way
✗ Wrong: You're on the 📦 Current Week tab. You upload a photo for "Teriyaki Salmon." The portal is showing Building Next dishes — your photo is saved but customers can't see it.

✓ Right: You switch to 🔜 Building Next first. The banner at the top confirms "Portal reads from this slot." You upload the photo — it appears on the customer portal immediately.
How to check: Each dish card has a coloured banner in its Portal section — green means "edits here go live on the portal," red means "wrong slot — customers won't see these changes." The tab banner at the top of the dish grid also tells you the portal status at a glance.
Advance Week

When the current week's orders are fulfilled and you're ready to move on, click ⏭ Advance Week (top-right of the Weekly Menu panel). This does four things atomically:

  • Archives current week — snapshots the menu, costs, and labour into Weekly Archive
  • Promotes Building Next → Current — the next week's dishes, week label, drop dates, and deadlines all move across
  • Clears the Building Next slot — next slot is empty and ready for the week after

How the Order List is Generated

The Purchasing page reads every dish in the weekly menu, works out the total raw ingredient weight needed (recipe quantity per serving × total portions × scale factors), and consolidates all ingredients across all dishes. It then groups the result by supplier.

Example: Chicken Breast across 3 dishes
Tikka & Rice Bowl: 165g raw × 40 portions = 6,600g
Broccoli Protein Bowl: 165g × 1.2 scale × 15 portions = 2,970g

PAR Level Warnings

If you've set a PAR level on an ingredient (minimum stock level in grams), the Purchasing page will highlight that row in amber if the required batch quantity falls below your PAR threshold. This means you either have enough in stock to cover the week, or you should consider ordering more than the minimum calculated.

PAR is a prompt, not a gate. The system doesn't know your actual stock levels — it only knows the threshold you set. PAR is designed to flag ingredients where running out would be critical (e.g. proteins, expensive items).
Supplier Grouping

Lines are grouped by the supplier field you set on each ingredient. If an ingredient has no supplier, it falls under "Misc". For the cleanest order list, ensure every ingredient has a supplier set. This makes it easy to call or email each supplier with a single consolidated order.

The order list only covers ingredients in your recipes. It does not include packaging materials, cleaning supplies, or any non-food items. Those should be managed separately. The Cost Stack is where you account for packaging costs financially, but the Purchasing page only handles food ingredients.

💾 Saving & Backups
How data is saved · The one golden rule · Backup routine · Moving to a new machine
How Data is Saved

All data saves automatically to your browser's localStorage — a private storage area tied to the specific browser and device you're using. Every action (adding, editing, deleting) saves immediately. There is no cloud sync, no account, and no server. The data lives only in the browser on the machine where you entered it.

localStorage is browser-specific. Data entered in Chrome will not appear in Firefox on the same machine, and vice versa. Data on your laptop will not sync to your phone. Pick one browser on one device as your permanent home for this system.
Dark / Light Mode

The app opens in dark mode by default. Click the 🌙 Dark mode toggle at the bottom of the left sidebar to switch to light mode. Your preference is saved in your browser and restored every time you open the file.

The One Golden Rule
Do not move or rename the HTML file once you've started entering data. localStorage keys are tied to the file path. If you move the file to a different folder or rename it, the browser treats it as a new file and shows an empty system. Your old data is not lost — move the file back to its original location and reload, and it will reappear.

The JSON export contains everything in the DB object: ingredients, recipes, menu dishes, weekly archives, production adjustments, cost items, labour staff and shifts, and all settings. What the Portal Is

The Customer Order Portal (index-order.html) is a separate, customer-facing page hosted on GitHub Pages. Customers browse the week's menu, choose their delivery day, pick dishes, enter their details, and place an order. Everything submits directly into Firebase — the same database the ops app uses — so orders appear in real time in the Orders page with no manual transfer needed.

Same Firebase, two apps. Both the ops app and the portal point to 29perfOS/data. The portal reads menu data (dishes, published status, drop dates, week label, deadline) and writes order data (orders + customers). The ops app controls all of that and is the single source of truth.

Weekly Setup Checklist — Step by Step

Follow this in order every week. Each step has a clear "when to do it" so nothing gets missed.

1
Switch to Building Next and build the menu  ▸ Do this Thursday, after Wednesday drop
Click the 🔜 Building Next tab in Weekly Menu. Add all dishes for the coming week. For each dish: set S1 and S2 portion counts (how many you plan to cook), assign the Fuel Line (Fuel Plus or Fuel Lite), upload the dish photo, fill in the "Serves with" field (e.g. "Turmeric Rice, Chow Chow Slaw"), and toggle any attribute tags (High Protein, Vegan, etc.).

Example: It's Thursday 10 Apr. You've just completed this week's Wednesday drop. You switch to Building Next and add 6 dishes for the week of 14–20 Apr — Teriyaki Salmon, Tikka Bowl, and 4 others — each with a photo, serves description, and 25 S1 / 20 S2 portions.
2
Set the week label  ▸ Do this at the same time as step 1
In the Week label field next to the slot tabs, type the week range (e.g. 14 Apr – 20 Apr). This label tags every order placed that week so you can identify them in the Orders page and Weekly Archive. Without a label, the portal will not allow you to open ordering.

Example: You type "14 Apr – 20 Apr" in the week label field while on Building Next. All customer orders placed this week will be tagged with this label.
3
Set delivery drop dates  ▸ Do this at the same time as step 1
In the Drop Dates strip below the slot tabs, pick the Sunday and Wednesday delivery dates. These dates appear on the customer portal session headers so customers know exactly when their order will arrive.

Example: You set Sunday = 14 Apr and Wednesday = 17 Apr. Customers on the portal see "Sunday 14th April" and "Wednesday 17th April" as their session choices, not just "Session 1 / Session 2."
4
Open the portal for orders  ▸ Do this Friday evening
In the portal strip at the top of the page, click Open for Orders. Then click ⚙ Deadlines and set a closing time for Sunday and Wednesday ordering (e.g. Saturday 20:00 for Sunday delivery, Tuesday 20:00 for Wednesday delivery). The portal auto-closes at that time and shows a countdown to customers. Status changes to 🟢 Open.

Example: It's Friday 11 Apr at 18:00. You click "Open for Orders" and set the Sunday deadline to Sat 12 Apr 20:00 and Wednesday deadline to Tue 15 Apr 20:00. Customers can now see and order next week's menu. The Sunday window closes automatically Saturday night.

Note: As soon as you added a dish to Building Next in step 1, customers could already see the new menu on the portal. Opening for orders is what lets them place and pay — the two are separate.
5
Confirm incoming orders  ▸ Do this Saturday, as orders come in
Go to the Orders page. New portal orders arrive with a 🟡 Pending badge. Review them and click ✓ Confirm to accept, or use Bulk Confirm to accept all at once. Check the allocation bar at the top of the Orders page — if a dish shows more orders than planned portions, increase its S1 or S2 count in the Weekly Menu to cover demand.

Example: By Saturday 10:00, you have 38 confirmed orders. The allocation bar shows "Teriyaki Salmon: 22 ordered / 20 planned" — it's over. You go to Building Next → Teriyaki Salmon and bump S2 from 20 to 25 to give yourself headroom.
6
Advance Week  ▸ Do this Thursday, after this Wednesday's drop
Once all orders for the current week are delivered and confirmed, click ⏭ Advance Week (top-right of the Weekly Menu panel). This archives the current week, promotes Building Next to Current, migrates all portal orders into the current week view, and clears Building Next so you can start the cycle again.

Example: It's Thursday 17 Apr. Wednesday's drop is done — all 38 orders delivered. You click Advance Week. "14 Apr – 20 Apr" is now your Current Week, all its orders are visible in Fulfillment. Building Next is empty, ready for you to build "21 Apr – 27 Apr."
Before advancing: Make sure all current-week orders are confirmed (not just pending). Ensure Building Next has a week label and at least one dish. Advance Week will block with an error if either condition isn't met.
